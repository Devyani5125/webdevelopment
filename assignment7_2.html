🏰 Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort is a historic hill fort located in the Raigad district of Maharashtra, India. It served as the capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s kingdom and is a symbol of Maratha pride.

The fort is situated at a height of approximately 2700 feet above sea level and is surrounded by deep valleys, making it naturally protected from enemies.

Raigad Fort is famous for the coronation of Shivaji Maharaj in the year 1674, marking the establishment of the Maratha Empire.

Important places inside the fort include:

  • Raj Bhavan (King's Palace)
  • Jagdishwar Temple
  • Market area (Bazarpeth)
  • Hirkani Buruj
  • Samadhi of Shivaji Maharaj

The fort has around 1737 steps to reach the top, and today a ropeway facility is also available for visitors.
